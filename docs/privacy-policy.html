1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how LinkedIn Gateway ("we", "our", or "the Extension") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our Chrome extension and related services.

LinkedIn Gateway is a productivity tool that helps you interact with LinkedIn and AI services through a secure proxy. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about our data practices.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 LinkedIn Integration

When you connect your LinkedIn account, we collect:

  • LinkedIn Session Cookies: Temporary authentication cookies that allow the Extension to make requests on your behalf.
  • LinkedIn Profile Information: Your public profile data (name, profile picture, headline) for display purposes.
  • CSRF Tokens: Security tokens required for authenticated requests.
We do NOT collect: Your LinkedIn password, private messages, or your connections' private information.

2.2 Gemini AI Integration

When you connect your Google account for Gemini AI features, we collect:

  • OAuth Access Tokens: Temporary tokens for Google AI services access.
  • Google Email Address: For identification and display purposes.
  • Profile Picture URL: For display in the Extension dashboard.
We do NOT collect: Your Google password, Gmail content, Google Drive files, or any Google Workspace data.

2.3 API Keys & Usage Data

  • Generated API Keys: Authenticate your requests to our backend services.
  • Request Logs: For security, debugging, and rate limiting (timestamps, endpoints, status codes).
  • Error Logs: Technical information to improve the service.
We do NOT collect: Browsing history, form data from other websites, personal files, or location data.

3. How We Use Your Information

  1. Provide Core Services: Enable LinkedIn API and Gemini AI features through our secure proxy.
  2. Authentication: Verify your identity and maintain secure sessions.
  3. Rate Limiting: Ensure fair usage and service quality.
  4. Security: Detect and prevent unauthorized access or abuse.
  5. Debugging: Identify and fix technical issues.
  6. Service Improvement: Analyze aggregated, non-personal usage patterns.

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Where Data is Stored

  • Local Storage: Session cookies, tokens, and preferences stored in your browser.
  • Backend Servers: API keys and session metadata on secured servers.
  • Database: PostgreSQL with encrypted connections.

4.2 Security Measures

  • üîê TLS 1.3 Encryption for all data in transit
  • üîê Encrypted Storage for sensitive credentials at rest
  • üîê API Key Hashing - we cannot retrieve your original key
  • üîê No Password Storage - we never have access to your passwords
  • üîê Session Timeouts - tokens expire and require re-authentication

4.3 Data Retention

  • Session Data: Cleared when you disconnect or uninstall.
  • API Logs: Retained 30 days, then automatically deleted.
  • Account Data: Deleted upon account deletion request.
